Common Questions

How do I manage or cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are managed through Apple, not through NexGenSpec.

Open iOS Settings → tap your name at the top → Subscriptions → NexGenSpec → tap Cancel Subscription. Cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period; you retain Pro access until then.

To change tier (Monthly ↔ Annual), tap the other plan in the same Subscriptions screen.

How do I delete my account?

Open NexGenSpec → Settings → Account → Delete Account. The app asks you to reauthenticate (password or Sign in with Apple), then permanently:

  • Deletes your Firebase authentication record.
  • Erases every inspection, photo, signature, and PDF stored on the device.
  • Saves a deletion receipt to your Files app under On My iPad → NexGenSpec → NexGenSpecReceipts so you have a record.

This action cannot be undone. NexGenSpec keeps no server-side copy of inspection content.

How do I export a finished inspection report?

Open the inspection → tap Finalize and collect both signatures → tap Invoice & Send. The app generates a branded PDF with your company logo on every page and opens a mail composer with the client and inspector copies attached.

You can also export the full inspection package (PDF + photos + LiDAR USDZ files) as a .zip via the Files app: tap the share icon on the inspection → Save to Files.

Do my inspections sync between iPads?

No. NexGenSpec is intentionally local-first — your inspection content never leaves your device. Inspections live on the iPad or iPhone they were created on.

To move an inspection between your own devices: open the inspection, tap the share icon, choose Save to Files, then transfer the resulting .zip via AirDrop, iCloud Drive, or any file-sharing method you prefer.

What if I cancel my subscription — do I lose past inspections?

No. Inspections you have already created remain on your device and stay readable and exportable. Subscription only gates the creation of new inspections beyond the free trial allowance and removes the watermark on freshly generated PDF reports.

The app crashed. What information should I send?

If the app crashed, anonymized crash reports are automatically sent to Firebase Crashlytics so we can fix the bug. These reports contain no inspection content or personal information.

It also helps if you email contact@nexgenspec.com with: the iPad/iPhone model, iOS version, NexGenSpec version, and a short description of what you were doing when the crash happened.

How do I back up my inspections?

NexGenSpec relies on iCloud Backup. Make sure iCloud Backup is turned on under iOS Settings → your name → iCloud → iCloud Backup. Your inspection data is included in the device backup.

For an explicit copy of a specific inspection, use Save to Files from the inspection's share menu to write a .zip archive to iCloud Drive (or any provider you choose).

Does NexGenSpec work without an internet connection?

Yes. Inspections are created, captured, finalized, and exported entirely on-device. Internet is only required for sign-in (one time per device), subscription validation, the weather stamp at the start of an inspection, and sending the final PDF via email.

If you go offline mid-inspection, the app continues to work. Pro entitlement is cached for up to 7 days offline so a subscriber on a remote job site does not lose Pro features.

Account & Privacy

NexGenSpec is local-first and stores all inspection content on your device. We do not host your reports, photos, or client information on our servers. See the Privacy Policy for the full data-handling story and the Terms of Service for usage terms.

System Requirements

  • iPad: iPadOS 17 or later. LiDAR room scanning requires iPad Pro 11" / 12.9" / 13" with LiDAR sensor.
  • iPhone: iOS 17 or later. LiDAR room scanning requires iPhone Pro or Pro Max with LiDAR sensor.
  • Storage: ~150 MB for the app itself, plus space for your inspections (typical inspection with 60–100 photos is 200–400 MB).
  • Network: Required for sign-in, subscription validation, weather stamp, and emailing reports. Not required during the inspection itself.
